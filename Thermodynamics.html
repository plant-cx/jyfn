Thermodynamics

Thermodynamics is the study of heat, energy, and how they move around and transform from one form to another. Its basically the rules that govern everything from engines to refrigerators to the entire universe. These laws are fundamental and they cant be broken - they describe what's physically possible and what isnt.

The Zeroth Law

The Zeroth Law sounds weird because it was added after the others but its so fundamental they had to give it priority. It says if two systems are each in thermal equilibrium with a third system then they're in thermal equilibrium with each other. Basically if object A and object B are both the same temperature as object C, then A and B are the same temperature as each other. This might seem obvious but it defines what temperature actually means and lets us use thermometers.

The First Law


The First Law is just conservation of energy applied to thermodynamics. It says the change in internal energy of a system equals the heat added to it minus the work done by it. The equation is ΔU = Q - W. This means energy cant be created or destroyed only converted between different forms. If you add heat to a gas it either increases the internal energy or does work by expanding, or both. You can't get more energy out than you put in which is why perpetual motion machines are impossible.

The Second Law

The Second Law is probably the most important and it has several ways of being stated. One version says heat naturally flows from hot to cold, never the other way around without doing work. Another version says the total entropy of an isolated system always increases or stays the same, never decreases. This law explains why some processes are irreversible and why we cant build 100% efficient heat engines.

What is Entropy?


Entropy is like a measure of disorder or randomness in a system. More technically its the number of ways you can arrange the molecules and still have the same macroscopic state. High entropy means lots of possible arrangements - lots of disorder. Low entropy means fewer arrangements - more order. The Second Law says entropy always increases which is why your room gets messy on its own but never cleans itself. Its why you cant unscramble an egg or unburn a piece of paper.

The Third Law

The Third Law says that as temperature approaches absolute zero (0 Kelvin or -273.15°C) the entropy of a perfect crystal approaches zero. Basically at absolute zero all molecular motion stops and theres only one possible arrangement so entropy is at its minimum. The important thing is you can never actually reach absolute zero - you can get really close but never quite there. This has been tested and the coldest temperatures achieved in labs are just fractions of a degree above absolute zero.

Heat Engines


Heat engines like car engines or power plants convert heat energy into mechanical work. They operate between a hot resevoir and a cold reservoir. The efficiency of any heat engine is limited by the Second Law - you cant convert all the heat into work because some must be rejected to the cold reservoir. The maximum possible efficiency is given by the Carnot cycle which depends only on the temperatures of the hot and cold reservoirs. Real engines are always less efficient than this theoretical maximum.

Refrigerators and Heat Pumps

Refrigerators and heat pumps are basically heat engines running backwards. Instead of converting heat to work they use work to move heat from cold to hot. Your fridge takes heat from inside and dumps it outside which is why the back of a fridge is warm. Heat pumps do the same thing to heat houses. They dont violate the Second Law because you're putting in work to make heat flow against its natural direction. The coefficient of performance tells you how efficiently they move heat compared to the work you put in.

The Heat Death of the Universe


The Second Law has deep implications for the entire universe. Since entropy always increases eventually the universe will reach maximum entropy - a state where everything is at the same temperature and no more useful work can be extracted. This is called the heat death. All the stars will burn out, all matter will decay, and everything will be at the same incredibly low temperature spread uniformly throughout space. Its a depressing thought but its billions and billions of years away so dont worry about it too much.
